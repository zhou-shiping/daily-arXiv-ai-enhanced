Our results provide a quantitative picture of the crossover between Markovian and non-Markovian dynamics on picosecond timescales and show how the finite bandwidth of the driving field limits the dynamically relevant bath spectrum. Furthermore, we demonstrate that thermodynamic quantities such as heat production can be inferred directly from the dynamics of an individual phonon mode, enabling their experimental measurement using time-resolved spectroscopy.
